# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Lucie and anyone she sends the link to — friends, family, and (since it's public at goldfishpond.vercel.app and she runs HIJINX, an AI-enablement consultancy) the occasional prospective client. Context of use: an idle browser tab, a coffee break, showing someone on a phone. Nobody arrives with a task; they arrive to poke water and watch fish. Session shape: 30 seconds of delight or 20 minutes of ambient company.

## Product Purpose

A zero-build browser terrarium: a painterly goldfish pond with fish you can feed, water you can poke, a dachshund who visits, a frog, day/night, and fully synthesized sound. It exists for joy — personal first, but kept portfolio-worthy. Success = someone smiles, leaves the tab open, and sends the link to somebody else.

## Brand Personality

Cozy, hand-made, quietly alive. A storybook garden, not an app. Everything is procedural and slightly imperfect on purpose — it should feel drawn and inhabited, never rendered or manufactured. The upcoming second art style is Canvascycle-authentic pixel art (Mark Ferrari: limited palette, dithering, palette-cycled water) — retro as *craft heritage*, not retro as joke.

## Anti-references

- **Gamified/busy**: no scores, achievements, streaks, HUD clutter, or notification energy. It is an ambient garden; UI whispers and recedes.
- **Sterile/minimal**: never flat corporate calm-app minimalism (Calm/Headspace gradient serenity, wellness-app blandness). Texture, character, and hand-made warmth win over cleanliness.
- Existing chrome is two quiet circular toggles top-right; new chrome must match that restraint.

## Design Principles

1. **The pond is the interface.** Chrome exists only at the edges and only when needed; nothing overlays the water except things that live in the world (fish, pads, pup).
2. **In-world over on-screen.** Prefer diegetic affordances (a cup on the coping, a hint that reads like a storybook whisper) to buttons and labels.
3. **Imperfect on purpose.** Procedural variation, asymmetry, and organic timing everywhere; nothing pixel-perfectly regular unless the pixel grid itself is the style.
4. **Craft is the flex.** As a quiet portfolio piece, quality shows in details (sound design, easing, palette discipline), never in self-promotion.
5. **Two styles, one garden.** Painted and 8-bit are the same pond, same inhabitants, same behavior — only the rendering language changes. Style-switching is a costume change, not a different product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target — it's a toy. Baseline: controls stay usable (real buttons, visible focus, sensible labels), text stays readable, and nothing strobes (palette-cycling stays slow and gentle). Audio is always optional and defaults to a polite unlock hint.
